CHAPTER 1: THE DEPARTMENTS IN DETAIL 

Department A — Email Command Center 

Mission. Every email answered within 30 minutes during business hours, 2 hours off hours. Every email tagged, categorized, and routed. No email lost. No broker ghosted. 

Reports to: Shirel, via the Shift Lead. 

Headcount: 2 workers covering 16–18 hours/day combined. 

How it works. The Email Commander checks the shared inbox every 15 minutes. They categorize: Deal Submission (routes to Data Intelligence), Broker Follow-Up (routes to Phone Ops), Investor Inquiry (routes to Adir), Administrative (handles directly), Spam (archives with tag). 

For deal submissions, the acknowledgment is immediate — but it’s not a cold auto-reply. The system acts like it KNOWS the broker. “Welcome back — this is your fifth submission. I see the property on Oak Street sold for $2.7M, nice result. Our research division is reviewing this one now. Expect your report within 72 hours.” The Email Commander pulls this from the broker’s CRM profile and their deal history. Every touchpoint feels personal. 

The Rejection Engine. 95% of deals don’t fit. Every rejection goes out personalized: “We reviewed 123 Main Street. At the current cap rate of 6.2%, it’s above our threshold for this submarket. If you have anything in the 8%+ range or any seller open to creative structures — seller financing, master lease, assumption — we want to see it immediately.” That turns every NO into a relationship deposit that brings the broker back with the right deal next time. 

AI workflow. The Commander pastes the incoming email plus the broker’s CRM profile into AI. AI drafts the response. Commander reviews, adjusts tone, sends. Over time, the department’s Shadow Brain LLM has seen thousands of emails and can draft 80% of responses autonomously — the Commander becomes a QA layer, not a writer. 

Vetting test. 10 sample emails (deal submissions, broker questions, investor inquiries, spam). 45 minutes. Candidate must triage all 10, draft responses, tag them. Evaluated on: speed, triage accuracy, response quality, institutional tone, and whether they used AI effectively.

How it works. The analyst inputs data into our customized Excel/Google Sheets model (per asset type). The model calculates automatically — but the analyst INTERPRETS. Does the NOI make sense? AI workflow. Analyst pastes data package and summary into Claude: “What am I missing? What risks aren’t in the numbers? CHAPTER 4: INCENTIVES & RETENTION 

Base Pay — No Deal Bonuses, Ever 

Per Lazear (Stanford), Ariely (Duke), and Pink: bonuses on complex cognitive work create gaming behavior and destroy intrinsic motivation. Instead: permanent milestone raises — every 6 months, base pay increases 10–15% if metrics are met. After 2 years, the worker earns 40–60% more than they started. Leaving becomes irrational. 

The AI Adoption Bonus 

Every quarter, each department head demonstrates what percentage of tasks are now AI-handled versus manual. Quarter 1 baseline: 100% manual. Quarter 2 target: 20% AI. Quarter 3: 40%. Quarter 4: 60%. 

Hitting target: 15% quarterly bonus. Exceeding: 25%. 

The worker who reaches 80%+ AI automation gets PROMOTED — manage a second department, train new hires, or build AI workflows for another function. Making yourself efficient through AI is the fastest path to higher pay. Resisting AI is the fastest path to replacement. 

The ultimate goal: a worker who builds their department to run 90%+ on AI sits on the beach and gets paid — because the machine works. That’s the mission, that’s the incentive, and that’s the culture. 

Phantom Equity (After 12–18 Months) 

For proven loyalty: 0.5–1% phantom equity. Back-weighted 4-year vest: 10%/20%/30%/40%. Leaving in year 2 or 3 is economically painful — the biggest chunk vests in year 4\. Combined with milestone raises, the golden handcuffs tighten progressively.
